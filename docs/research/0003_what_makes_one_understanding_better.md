# Research Paper 0003
# What Makes One Understanding Better Than Another?

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Working Paper  
**Milestone:** 0.3

## Purpose

Change alone does not imply improvement.

This paper asks:

> **What makes one understanding preferable to another?**

## Candidate Quality Dimensions

- Greater coherence
- Greater explanatory power
- Greater explicitness of assumptions
- Better conceptual distinctions
- Greater consequence awareness
- Better characterised uncertainty
- Greater integration of diverse perspectives

## Principle

Recognising ignorance can represent progress. Agreement is neither necessary nor sufficient for better understanding.

## Acceptance Criteria

The model can justify why one Understanding State represents an improvement—or explain why it does not.

## Open Questions

- Should quality be multi-dimensional?
- Can improvements be incomparable?
- Can understanding improve in different directions simultaneously?
